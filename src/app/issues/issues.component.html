Infertility in women can occur due to various hormonal, structural, or lifestyle factors. Common causes include:

  • Ovulation Disorders

    Irregular or absent ovulation due to PCOS, thyroid imbalance, or hormonal dysfunction.

  • Poor Egg Quality or Low Ovarian Reserve (Low AMH)

    Decline in egg number or quality, often due to age or premature ovarian failure.

  • Blocked Fallopian Tubes

    Often caused by pelvic infections, endometriosis, or previous surgeries.

  • Endometriosis

    A condition where uterine tissue grows outside the uterus, affecting egg release and implantation.

  • Uterine or Cervical Problems

    Fibroids, polyps, or abnormalities that prevent implantation or cause miscarriages.

  • Lifestyle Factors

    Stress, obesity, smoking, or excessive caffeine intake can reduce fertility potential.
